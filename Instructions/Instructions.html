The Experiment - Introduction

    In this experiment, you will make several fictitious purchasing decisions. Though there's no physical product, your choices will have real consequences. Based on your decisions, you can earn a bonus of up to €31.32 and help the environment by planting trees in a location of your choice.

    One random participant will be selected after the experiment to receive the bonus. Rest assured, you can't lose money during this experiment.

Purchasing Decisions

    You will make 54 purchasing decisions over 54 rounds . In each round, you will see 2 products with specific characteristics.

    You'll make purchasing decisions based on these characteristics. While the products aren't real, the benefits of your decisions are. More sustainable choices can lead to a higher bonus. The next page will explain the product characteristics.

Product Characteristics

Info:

    Each round presents two products of the same type (bananas, onions, or apples) with information on their label, price, and sustainability.

Characteristics:
  • Label:
    Indicates which authority approved the product. There are two types:

    Rated from 1 to 3 leaves (), with more leaves indicating higher sustainability ().
