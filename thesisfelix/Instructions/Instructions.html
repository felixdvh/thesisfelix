The Experiment - In short

  • In this experiment, you will make several fictitious purchasing decisions.
  • Though there's no physical product, your choices will have real consequences.
  • Based on your decisions, you can earn a payment of up to €25 .
  • Based on your decisions, a maximum of 17 trees can be planted.
  • At the end of the experiment, one random participant will be selected to receive the payment.
  • Additionally, trees will be planted in their prefered location.
  • Rest assured, you can't lose money during this experiment.
  • The trees will be planted by: .

    • Would you like to choose where the trees would be planted?

    Purchasing Decisions

  • You'll start with an endowment of €25 .
  • With the endowment you need to buy products.
  • There will be 54 rounds .
  • In each round, you will see 2 products .
  • You'll make 1 purchase per round.
  • The products differ in certain characteristics.
  • The products aren't real, but the benefits of your decisions are.
  • The next page will explain the characteristics and benefits.

    • Press → to continue reading

    Product Characteristics

    Info:
  • Each round presents two products of the same type (bananas, onions, or apples).
  • The products differ in certain characteristics.

    • Characteristics:
  • Label: Indicates which authority approved the information. There are two types:

      (1) Government label: , (2) Commercial label: .

  • Price: Varies between three types of information:

      (1) Selling price (€/kg), (2) Price structure (€/kg), (3) Price rating (1-3, where 1 is cheap and 3 is expensive).

    Higher price → lower payment.

  • Sustainability: Rated from 1 to 3 leaves (), with 3 leaves being most sustainable.

    Lower sustainability → less trees planted.
